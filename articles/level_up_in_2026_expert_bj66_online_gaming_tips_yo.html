Mastering Bankroll Management

Bankroll management is your safety net and your growth engine. Most promising players fail here, not through lack of talent but through poor money control.

Define Your Bankroll

Your bankroll is money set aside exclusively for gaming—no rent, no bills, no emergencies. Treat it like business capital, not pocket money.

- Never gamble with funds you can’t afford to lose. - Track deposits and withdrawals separately from everyday spending. - Keep a written or digital log of every session: stakes, results, and notes.

Use Percentage-Based Staking

In 2026’s swing-heavy environment, fixed high stakes are dangerous. A percentage-based staking model adjusts automatically to your current bankroll.

For example:

- Conservative: stake 1–2% of bankroll per session - Moderate: stake 3–5% per session (for experienced, disciplined players)

When your bankroll grows, your stakes increase safely. When it shrinks, your stakes reduce automatically, helping you avoid catastrophic wipeouts.

Set Stop-Loss and Stop-Win Limits

Decide in advance:

- Maximum loss per day (e.g., 5–10% of bankroll) - Reasonable daily or session profit target

Walk away when you hit either. Protecting yesterday’s profit is as important as chasing tomorrow’s.
