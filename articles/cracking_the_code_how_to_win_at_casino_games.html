Understanding the House Edge

Every casino game is designed with a built-in advantage for the house, known as the house edge. This percentage represents the average amount the casino expects to win from each wager in the long run. Different games have varying house edges. For example, blackjack, with skillful play, often has a lower house edge than slot machines. Understanding the house edge is crucial for making informed decisions about which games to play and how to manage your bankroll. Before placing a bet, research the specific game's house edge and consider your risk tolerance.
