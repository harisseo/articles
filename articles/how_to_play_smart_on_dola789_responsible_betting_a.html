Fundamental Blackjack Strategy for 2026

The core of winning blackjack remains basic strategy—choosing the action that statistically loses the least (or wins the most) over time, given your hand and the dealer’s upcard.

#### 1. When to Hit or Stand

General guidelines (for a typical multi-deck game where dealer stands on soft 17):

- Hard totals (no Ace counted as 11) - Always hit 8 or less. - Hit 9 unless dealer shows 3–6 and you plan to double. - On 10 or 11, usually double against lower dealer cards; hit if dealer shows 10/A and you can’t double. - Stand on 12–16 if dealer shows 2–6, hit if dealer shows 7–Ace (with some detailed exceptions). - Always stand on 17 or more.

- Soft totals (Aces counted as 11) - With A2–A6, usually hit; sometimes double against 4–6. - With A7, stand against dealer 2, 7, 8; double vs 3–6; hit vs 9–Ace. - With A8 or A9, almost always stand (blackjack rules can slightly tweak this).

The exact matrix can be memorized over time, but the guiding idea: be more aggressive (hit/double) when the dealer is strong, and more conservative (stand) when the dealer is weak.

#### 2. When to Double Down

Doubling is one of your biggest profit opportunities:

- Double on 10 vs dealer 2–9 (hit vs 10/A). - Double on 11 vs dealer 2–10 (hit vs A). - Double on 9 vs dealer 3–6 (hit otherwise).

For soft hands, double more often when the dealer has a weak card (4–6) and you have A2–A7.

#### 3. When to Split Pairs

Think of splitting as creating two hands with better starting positions:

- Always split: - Aces (A, A) - 8s (8, 8)

- Never split: - 5s (5, 5) – treat as 10, often double instead - 10s (10, 10) – you already have a strong hand

- Conditional splits: - 2s, 3s, 7s vs dealer 2–7 - 6s vs dealer 2–6 - 9s vs dealer 2–6 and 8–9 (stand vs 7, 10, A)

Using splits correctly dramatically improves your results over the long run.

#### 4. Should You Take Insurance?

From a purely mathematical standpoint, never take insurance in standard play. It’s a side bet with a high house edge. The only exception is extremely advanced card counting scenarios, which most recreational players shouldn’t rely on online.
