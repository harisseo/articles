Once you've mastered the fundamentals and developed a solid strategy, you can start incorporating advanced techniques to further improve your game.

* Bluffing Effectively: Bluffing is a crucial part of poker, but it should be used sparingly and strategically. Choose your spots carefully, and only bluff when you have a good reason to believe that your opponent will fold.

* Semi-Bluffing: Semi-bluffing is when you bet or raise with a hand that isn't the best but has the potential to improve. This puts pressure on your opponent while also giving you a chance to win the pot if you hit your draw.

* Floating: Floating is when you call a bet on the flop with a weak hand, intending to take the pot away on a later street. This can be effective against aggressive players who frequently continuation bet.

* Pot Control: Pot control is the art of managing the size of the pot. Sometimes, you'll want to keep the pot small, and other times, you'll want to build it up. Understanding when to do each is crucial for maximizing your profits.
