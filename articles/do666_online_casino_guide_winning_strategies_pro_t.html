RTP, House Edge & Volatility: The Numbers That Matter

Winning strategies start with math, not superstition. Three metrics should guide your game selection.

#### Return to Player (RTP)

- RTP is the percentage of wagered money a game is expected to pay back over the long term. - A slot with 97% RTP, for example, theoretically returns 97 units for every 100 wagered over a very long sample size. - For better results over time, prioritize games with higher RTP (e.g., slots above 96%, blackjack variants around 99% with optimal play).

#### House Edge

- House edge is simply 100% minus the RTP. - A game with 2% house edge is statistically “better” than one with 6% house edge. - Your strategic goal is to consistently choose games and rulesets that minimize the house edge.

#### Volatility (Variance)

- Low volatility: frequent small wins, fewer extreme swings; good for maintaining bankroll and fulfilling wagering requirements. - High volatility: rarer but larger payouts; better for chasing big jackpots but riskier. - Match volatility to your bankroll size and risk tolerance.
