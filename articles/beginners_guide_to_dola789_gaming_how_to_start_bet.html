Understanding How Online Blackjack Works

Blackjack is a card game where you play against the dealer, not against other players. Your goal is simple: get a hand total closer to 21 than the dealer without going over (busting).

Key points:

- Number cards (2–10) are worth their face value. - Face cards (J, Q, K) are each worth 10. - Aces can count as 1 or 11, depending on which helps your hand more. - You win if: - Your total is higher than the dealer’s without busting, or - The dealer busts and you don’t, or - You get blackjack (an Ace + 10-value card) and the dealer doesn’t.

#### When to Double Down

Doubling is powerful because it increases your bet when the odds are in your favor.

Common guidelines:

- Double on 11 against dealer 2–10. - Double on 10 against dealer 2–9. - Double on 9 against dealer 3–6. - If doubling isn’t allowed by the table rules, just hit.

#### Playing Soft Hands (Hands with an Ace as 11)

“Soft” hands are more flexible because an Ace can be counted as 1 if you would bust.

- Soft 19 or 20 (A+8, A+9): Usually stand. - Soft 18 (A+7): - Stand vs dealer 2, 7, 8. - Double vs 3–6 (if allowed). - Hit vs 9, 10, Ace. - Soft 17 or less (A+2 to A+6): - Generally hit, except: - Double soft 13–18 vs dealer 4–6 when rules permit.

#### When to Split Pairs

Splitting can turn a weak hand into two profitable ones.

Common rules of thumb:

- Always split Aces and 8s. - Never split 5s or 10s. - Split 2s and 3s vs dealer 4–7 (sometimes 2–3 as well, depending on chart). - Split 6s vs dealer 3–6. - Split 7s vs dealer 2–7. - Split 9s vs dealer 2–6 and 8–9 (but stand vs 7, 10, Ace in many charts).

These guidelines are a starting point. For best results, study a full strategy chart that matches your table’s specific rules and practice until the decisions feel automatic.
