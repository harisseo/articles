The House Edge: Your Unseen Opponent

The house edge is the inherent advantage the casino has over players in any given game. It’s expressed as a percentage and represents the average amount the casino expects to win from each bet placed. For example, a game with a 2% house edge means that, on average, the casino will retain $2 for every $100 wagered. Knowing the house edge of different games is crucial for choosing those that offer the best odds. Games like blackjack and video poker, when played with optimal strategy, often have lower house edges compared to games like slots. Probability and Expected Value: Predicting the Odds

Probability is the likelihood of a specific outcome occurring. In casino games, understanding probability helps you assess the chances of winning a particular bet. Expected value (EV) is a calculation that combines probability with the potential payout of a bet. It represents the average amount you can expect to win or lose per bet in the long run. A positive EV suggests a potentially profitable bet, while a negative EV indicates a losing bet. While a positive EV is rare in casino games (as the house always has an edge), understanding how to calculate EV can help you make more informed betting decisions and avoid sucker bets.
