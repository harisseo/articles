Choose the Right Blackjack Tables for Bonus Play

Not all 21 tables are equal. The “best” table for fun is not always the “best” table for grinding through wagering efficiently.

Watch These Critical Rules

Look for rules that reduce house edge:

- Dealer stands on soft 17 (S17) instead of hitting (H17) - Double down on any 2 cards (not just 9–11) - Doubling after split allowed - Surrender option (especially late surrender) - Fewer decks (e.g., 1–6 decks vs 8+)

Avoid tables that have:

- Blackjack pays 6:5 instead of 3:2 – this massively increases house edge. - No doubling after split – reduces your ability to maximize strong hands. - High minimums with tight max-bet limits while you’re on a bonus.
