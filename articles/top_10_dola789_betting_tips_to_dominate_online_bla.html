4. Design a Smart, Data-Driven Betting Strategy

Basic strategy tells you what action to take; betting strategy dictates how much to wager.

Flat betting vs. progression

- Flat betting: same bet every hand. Safest, easiest, and compatible with bonus clearing. - Positive progression: increase bets after wins (e.g., 1–2–3 units). Helps press hot streaks but doesn’t change the house edge. - Negative progression (Martingale, etc.): increase after losses. Risky and can crush a bankroll when variance hits.

In 2026, most successful players will favor a hybrid:

- Mostly flat betting for stability - Controlled increases during clear upswings within defined limits (e.g., cap bet at 4–5× base stake)

Track performance and adjust

Keep simple records:

- Average bet - Max drawdown (worst downswing) - Number of hands per session

This data helps you calibrate your base bet and risk tolerance over time.
