Falling for Gambler's Fallacy

The gambler's fallacy is the mistaken belief that past events influence future independent events. For example, believing that a roulette wheel is "due" for a red outcome after a series of black spins is a classic example. Each spin is independent, and the odds remain the same regardless of previous results. Recognizing and avoiding this fallacy is crucial for making rational betting decisions.
