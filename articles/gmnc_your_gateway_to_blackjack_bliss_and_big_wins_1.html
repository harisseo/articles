The Basics: Understanding the Rules of Blackjack

At its heart, blackjack is a comparison game between the player and the dealer. The objective is to have a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer, without exceeding 21 (busting). Numbered cards hold their face value, face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 10, and Aces can be worth either 1 or 11, depending on which value benefits the hand more.

A standard game begins with each player and the dealer receiving two cards. One of the dealer's cards is dealt face down (the "hole card"), adding an element of uncertainty. Players then have several options:

* Hit: Request another card to increase the hand's total value. * Stand: Decline to take any more cards, ending their turn. * Double Down: Double the initial bet and receive one more card only. This is typically done when a player has a strong hand, such as a total of 11. * Split: If the player's first two cards are a pair, they can split them into two separate hands, each requiring an additional bet equal to the original. * Surrender: In some casinos, players can surrender their hand and receive half of their bet back. This is usually done when the player has a very weak hand against a strong dealer upcard.

Once all players have made their decisions, the dealer reveals their hole card. The dealer must then follow specific rules, typically hitting until their hand totals 17 or more, and standing on 17 or higher. If the player's hand is closer to 21 than the dealer's, the player wins. If the player busts, they lose their bet. If the dealer busts, all remaining players win. A tie (a "push") results in the player's bet being returned.
